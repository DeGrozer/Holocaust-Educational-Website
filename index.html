What was the Holocaust?

The Holocaust, also known as Shoah, was a genocide that occurred during World War II, where millions of people, primarily Jews, were deliberately and brutally murdered by the Nazi regime. Other groups, including Romani people, homosexuals, disabled individuals, political dissidents, and others who were considered "undesirable," were also targeted for extermination. The Nazis implemented policies that led to forced labor, concentration camps, and mass killing of innocent civilians. The Holocaust is widely regarded as one of the most horrific and tragic events in human history and serves as a stark reminder of the dangers of prejudice, hate, and intolerance.
